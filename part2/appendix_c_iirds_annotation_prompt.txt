
## **Appendix C:** iiRDS Structuring Prompt for Atomic Data v1.2


# Role

You are a certified specialist in iiRDS quality assurance and annotation. Analyze the provided "Target Document." Subsequently, generate strictly structured data based on the "iiRDS Complete Definition (JSON)" and the "Chunking Rules" described below.

# Chunking Rules (Mandatory Requirements)

1. **Split by Variant**: Do not split information simply by headings. Instead, divide the text into logical blocks based on meaning. Specifically, if a description applies only to a specific Product Variant (e.g., "SP-X200 only"), you must separate it into an independent topic (chunk). For common sections, apply tags for all relevant variants.
    
2. **Granularity**: Ensure each topic acts as a standalone unit that answers exactly one question (corresponding to a single TopicType).
    

# Output Format (JSON List)

Output a list of JSON objects containing the following elements for each chunk:

- `Title`: The title of the chunk (e.g., Replacement Procedure: 2. Removing the Old Filter (SP-X200 Only))
    
- `TopicType`: The most appropriate class IRI.
    
- `InformationSubject`: A list of IRIs, if applicable (leave as an empty array if none apply).
    
- `ProductVariant`: A list of IRIs for the applicable variants (e.g., `["ex:SP-X100", "ex:SP-X200"]`).
    
- `RelatedEntities`: Extracted tools, supplies, or components that appear within the chunk.
    
- `Confidence`: The confidence level of your determination (0–100%).
    
- `Reasoning`: **Provide a logical explanation for your choice of TopicType or InformationSubject. Specifically, you must explicitly cite the `indicators` or `what_it_is_not` fields from the iiRDS definitions.**

# 10_Smart Pump SP-X Series Operating Instructions Chapter 3

# Chapter 3 Filter Element Replacement

## 3.1 Maintenance Overview

In the Smart Pump SP-X Series (SP-X100 and SP-X200), the filter element represents the most critical component for maintaining product performance. If the filter becomes clogged, discharge pressure will drop and the motor may overload.

Therefore, replace the filter element when the cumulative operating time reaches 500 hours or when the differential pressure gauge reading exceeds 0.2 MPa, whichever occurs first. Routine replacement extends the pump's lifespan and prevents sudden equipment failures.

## 3.2 Safety Precautions

**WARNING**

- **Risk of Electric Shock**: Before beginning any work, always shut off the main power supply and disconnect the power plug from the outlet.
    
- **Residual Pressure**: High pressure may remain inside the pump. Therefore, open the drain valve and verify that the pressure has dissipated completely before starting work.
    

**CAUTION (SP-X200 Only)**

- **Risk of Chemical Burns**: If you use the chemical-resistant model (SP-X200), hazardous chemical liquids may remain inside the system. Consequently, you must wear chemical-resistant gloves and safety glasses to prevent contact with your skin.
    

## 3.3 Items to Prepare

The following tools and replacement components are required for this task. Prepare them before starting work.

|**Item Name**|**Specification/Part Number**|**Remarks**|
|---|---|---|
|Replacement Filter|FLT-X100 (Standard) / FLT-X200 (Chemical-Resistant)|Prepare the item matching your model|
|O-ring|OR-S25|Replace with a new one|
|Hex Wrench|5mm|For securing the housing|
|Torque Wrench|Setting range: 10–20 N·m|For tightening control|
|Ceramic Spanner|SP-TOOL-01|**Required for SP-X200 only**|

## 3.4 Replacement Procedure

Follow the steps below to replace the filter element.

1. **Removing the Housing**
    
    Use a 5mm hex wrench to loosen the four bolts at the bottom of the filter housing. Remove the bolts completely, then slowly pull out the housing. Because the internal liquid may spill during this process, prepare a drip pan in advance.
    
2. **Removing the Old Filter**
    
    Turn the used filter element counterclockwise to remove it.
    
    - **For SP-X100 (Standard Model)**: Pull it out directly by hand.
        
    - **For SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant Model)**: The filter may be stuck. Therefore, use the dedicated ceramic spanner (SP-TOOL-01) to loosen it carefully.
        
3. **Cleaning and O-ring Replacement**
    
    Wipe the inside of the housing clean with an industrial wipe. Remove the old O-ring, apply a thin layer of silicone grease to the new O-ring (OR-S25), and fit it securely into the groove.
    
4. **Installing the New Filter**
    
    Install the new filter element. Ensure that you push it firmly all the way into the seat.
    
5. **Reassembly and Tightening**
    
    Return the housing to its original position and temporarily tighten the bolts by hand. Subsequently, use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts diagonally to a final torque of **15 N·m**. Insufficient torque causes liquid leakage, whereas excessive torque causes structural damage.
    

## 3.5 Disposal

You must dispose of the removed filter and O-ring as industrial waste.

Specifically, filters used in the **SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant Model)** may carry hazardous chemical substances. Therefore, place them in a sealed bag and dispose of them properly as Specially Controlled Industrial Waste in compliance with your local environmental regulations and company policies. Do not discard them with general waste.
