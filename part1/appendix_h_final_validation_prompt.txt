# 10_Smart Pump SP-X Series Instruction Manual Chapter 3
# Chapter 3: Replacement of the Filter Element

## 3.1 Maintenance Overview
For the Smart Pump SP-X Series (SP-X100 and SP-X200), the filter element is the most critical component for maintaining product performance. If the filter becomes clogged, it may cause a drop in discharge pressure or an overload on the motor.
Therefore, the recommended replacement interval is either when the cumulative operating time reaches 500 hours or when the differential pressure gauge reading exceeds 0.2 MPa, whichever comes first. Regular replacement extends the lifespan of the pump and prevents sudden failures.

## 3.2 Safety Precautions

**Warning**
* **Risk of Electric Shock:** Before starting work, you must turn off the main power and unplug the power plug from the outlet.
* **Residual Pressure:** High pressure may remain inside the pump. Consequently, you must open the drain valve and confirm that the pressure is completely released before performing any work.

**Caution (SP-X200 Only)**
* **Risk of Chemical Burns:** If you are using the Chemical-Resistant Model (SP-X200), dangerous chemical liquids may remain inside. You must wear chemical-resistant gloves and protective goggles to prevent adhesion to the skin.

## 3.3 Required Tools and Preparation

You require the following tools and replacement parts for this task. Prepare them before starting the work.

| Item Name | Specification / Part Number | Remarks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Replacement Filter | FLT-X100 (Standard) / FLT-X200 (Chemical-Resistant) | Prepare the one matching your model. |
| O-ring | OR-S25 | Replace with a new one. |
| Hex Wrench | 5mm | For fixing the housing. |
| Torque Wrench | Setting range 10-20 N・m | For tightening management. |
| Ceramic Spanner | SP-TOOL-01 | **Required for SP-X200 only.** |

## 3.4 Replacement Procedure

Follow the procedure below to perform the replacement.

1.  **Removing the Housing**
    Using a 5mm hex wrench, loosen the four bolts at the bottom of the filter housing. Completely remove the bolts and slowly pull out the housing. Because internal liquids may spill, prepare a drain pan.

2.  **Removing the Old Filter**
    Turn the used filter element counterclockwise to remove it.
    * **For SP-X100 (Standard Model):** Pull it straight out by hand.
    * **For SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant Model):** The filter might be stuck. Therefore, use the dedicated ceramic spanner (SP-TOOL-01) to carefully loosen it.

3.  **Cleaning and O-ring Replacement**
    Wipe the inside of the housing with a clean cloth. Remove the old O-ring, apply a thin layer of silicone grease to the new O-ring (OR-S25), and then install it into the groove.

4.  **Installing the New Filter**
    Attach the new filter element. Ensure that you insert it fully to the back.

5.  **Assembly and Tightening**
    Return the housing to its original position and temporarily tighten the bolts by hand. Then, use a torque wrench to fully tighten them diagonally to a torque of **15 N・m**. Insufficient tightening torque causes liquid leakage, while excessive torque causes damage.

## 3.5 Disposal
You must dispose of the removed used filter and O-ring as industrial waste.
Specifically, the filter used in the **SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant Model)** may have harmful chemicals attached to it. Therefore, you must place it in a sealed bag and dispose of it properly as specially controlled industrial waste, strictly following your local environmental regulations and internal company rules. Never dispose of it as general garbage.

## 3.6 Main Specifications (Reference)

| Item | SP-X100 (Standard) | SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Filter Filtration Accuracy | 10 µm | 5 µm |
| Max Operating Pressure | 1.0 MPa | 1.5 MPa |
| Allowable Liquid Temperature | 0 to 60°C | 0 to 90°C |
| Body Material | SUS304 | PTFE Lining |
