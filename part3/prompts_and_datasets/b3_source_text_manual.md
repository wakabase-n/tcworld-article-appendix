## Chapter 3: Filter Element Replacement

### 3.1 Maintenance Overview
The filter element represents the most critical component for maintaining product performance in the Smart Pump SP-X Series (SP-X100 and SP-X200). If the filter clogs, it can cause a drop in discharge pressure or place an overload on the motor.
Therefore, operators must replace the filter element when cumulative operating time reaches 500 hours, or when the differential pressure gauge exceeds 0.2 MPa, whichever occurs first. Regular replacement directly extends pump lifespan and prevents sudden equipment failures.

### 3.2 Safety Precautions

**WARNING**
* **Risk of Electric Shock:** Before starting work, you must turn off the main power and unplug the power plug from the outlet.
* **Residual Pressure:** High pressure may remain inside the pump. Therefore, you must open the drain valve and confirm that the pressure is completely released before performing any work.

**CAUTION (SP-X200 Only)**
* **Risk of Chemical Burns:** Because the chemical-resistant model (SP-X200) handles hazardous liquids, dangerous chemical substances may remain inside the equipment. You must wear chemical-resistant gloves and protective goggles to prevent adhesion to the skin.

### 3.3 Required Items

This work requires the following tools and replacement components. Prepare these items before starting the procedure.

| Item Name | Specification / Part Number | Remarks |
| :-------- | :----------------------------- | :-------------- |
| Replacement Filter | FLT-X100 (Standard) / FLT-X200 (Chemical-Resistant) | Prepare the item matching your specific model. |
| O-ring | OR-S25 | Replace with a new component. |
| Hex Wrench | 5mm | For housing fixation. |
| Torque Wrench | Setting Range: 10–20 N·m | For tightening control. |
| Ceramic Spanner | SP-TOOL-01 | **Required for SP-X200 only.** |

### 3.4 Replacement Procedure

Perform the replacement according to the following procedure:

1. **Removing the Housing**
Using a 5mm hex wrench, loosen the four bolts at the bottom of the filter housing. Completely remove the bolts and slowly pull out the housing. Because internal liquids may spill, prepare a drain pan to catch them.

2. **Removing the Old Filter**
Turn the used filter element counterclockwise to remove it.
* **For the SP-X100 (Standard Model):** Pull it straight out by hand.
* **For the SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant Model):** The filter element might be stuck. Therefore, use the dedicated ceramic spanner (SP-TOOL-01) to carefully loosen it.

3. **Cleaning and O-ring Replacement**
Wipe the inside of the housing with a clean cloth. Remove the old O-ring, apply a thin layer of silicone grease to the new O-ring (OR-S25), and then install it into the groove.

4. **Installing the New Filter**
Attach the new filter element. Ensure that you insert it fully to the back.

5. **Assembly and Tightening**
Return the housing to its original position and temporarily tighten the bolts by hand. Then, use a torque wrench to fully tighten them diagonally to a torque of **15 N·m**. Insufficient tightening torque causes liquid leakage, while excessive torque causes component damage.

### 3.5 Disposal
Operators must dispose of the removed filter elements and O-rings as industrial waste.
Specifically, filters used in the **SP-X200 (Chemical-Resistant Model)** may harbor hazardous chemical substances. Therefore, you must place them into a sealed bag and dispose of them properly as specially controlled industrial waste in strict accordance with your regional environmental regulations and internal corporate rules. Do not discard them as general waste.
